Muxe

January 2023 - September 2023

Muxe is a 2D platformer created at Gobelins for our narrative design course. We opted for a biopunk direction, a relatively unexplored science fiction genre. Biopunk is similar to cyberpunk, but instead of focusing on technological enhancements, humanity chooses to direct its research toward biological improvements. This genre allowed us to design various upgrades that the player could acquire, as well as a society built around the principle of symbiosis.
Here is the synopsis: In the 15th century, after the transhumanist wars, major philosophical and scientific movements evolved thanks to bioengineering and phytotechnology, causing a radical neo-ecological transition. Called phytotek, this technology allows the modification of the human body by adding plant characteristics. Bark skin, leaf hair, extra arms, or simple aesthetic enhancements—nothing is impossible. The secrets of phytotek are lost to most of the population. Alternative societies have emerged, whose members are systematically augmented. Muxe, a 15-year-old girl, must undergo her assignment: an ancestral ritual through which a member of her tribe receives their plant augmentations. As a farmer's daughter, Muxe's augmentations will help her work the fields like her parents. The ritual is a failure; it is Muxe's eyes that mutate. Strange flowers grow on each of the girl's eyes, allowing her to see the reality of her world: the city she lives in is actually built using the bodies of its inhabitants fused with wood. Now ostracized by society, Muxe flees the city in search of her brother, who disappeared three years earlier.
