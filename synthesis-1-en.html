All That Glitters

2024 - 2025

All That Glitters" is a project initiated in 2023 at ENJMIN. This game follows Isobel, a sixty-year-old woman living in seclusion as a gold prospector in an isolated forest. Her discovery of a talking stuffed fish and a penguin-shaped walkie-talkie leads her to learn that humans have been replaced by a canine civilization. While exploring, Isobel collects items that gradually reveal the secrets of this new society, all the while getting unintentionally involved in its evolution.

Together with three former classmates, we enriched the gameplay by adding mini-games, including ones centered around fishing and gold prospecting. I designed the gameplay for the fishing mini-game, developed a random item drop system linked to dialogues using Yarn Spinner, and handled all the audio, including sound design and music. Using Reaper and Fmod, we created an adaptive soundtrack that enhances immersion.

The game will be available on Steam in the first quarter of 2025. Feel free to add it to your wishlist!

Le Baratineur

2023

Le Baratineur is a narrative adventure game set in the 1970s in Marseille. The player takes on the role of a young, ambitious, and charismatic man, a master of persuasion, navigating through iconic landscapes of Provence. The game combines exploration, where players collect cards and arguments, and rhetorical confrontations based on real techniques and cognitive biases, with multiple possible outcomes. A true homage to Marseille and the French Connection, this project, designed as a graduation project for the specialized master's degree at Gobelins, was created by a team of four and resulted in an innovative prototype presented and showcased at the FLIP Festival 2023.

Riri & Coco

2023

Riri and Coco is a game designed in 48 hours during the Global Game Jam Var, based on the theme of "Duality," by a team of six multidisciplinary members. This cooperative two-player game combines puzzle-solving and quick reflexes, where Riri and Coco must solve riddles and progress through 11 randomly generated rooms before time runs out. Awarded the Murex prize, the game was showcased at the Digital and Creative Cultures Festival in Toulon in November 2022, as well as at Indie Game Lyon 2023. The team continued developing it to improve its stability and gameplay, and the first version is available on Itch.io.

Prototypes Interactifs

2023

During my internship with Gobelins and the Palais de la Découverte, I had the opportunity to create several interactive prototypes. Here are a few of them:

  • Space-Time Waves: This astronomy device is a small touch-screen game where you have to guide a photon to a planet. To do this, you place spheres of different masses on the screen, which deform the space-time grid and alter the photon's trajectory.
  • The Butterfly's Wingbeat: This was a haptic experience where you could feel the wingbeat of a butterfly as it landed on your finger when you made a gesture. The device was developed as part of the research and development on interactive interfaces, which I was responsible for at the Cité des Sciences. For this, I used several technologies, such as Ultrahaptics and a 3D screen without glasses.
  • The Interactive Aquarium: This was one of our projects at Gobelins, where we projected a scene filled with statues on the ceiling. These statues would light up with beams of painted light when someone entered the room, using an Arduino.
