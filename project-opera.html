Opera's Augmented Reality Guide

March 2021 - June 2021

This project is part of the research program “carried” by the Toulon Provence Méditerranée Superior School of Art and Design. The project is a mobile application in augmented reality, which presents the history and cultural aspect of the Opera house of Toulon, it is an interactive, sensory, emotional and immersive guide. This guide focuses on five paintings each illustrating an opera (The Flying Dutchman by Wagner, Carmen by Bizet, The Tales of Hoffmann by Offenbach, Spring Sonata by Beethoven, and Faust by Gounod). Our goal was to create an artistic experience for each painting in augmented reality while learning more about the opera. When you scan a painting with your phone, an augmented reality performance plays throughout the room. This performance is accompanied by the music of the corresponding opera played by the Montreal Philharmonic Ensemble on which the different visual animations are synchronized. At the end of each scene, different information about the painting and the opera are revealed. To make it more fun, we have developed several mini games related to the opera piece. A “liar's game” for the painting on "Carmen" or a gondola race for "The Tales of Hoffmann".

This is one of the most versatile projects I have ever worked on. It mixes both the more traditional arts like opera with more emerging arts. What I really liked about this project was the creative freedom we were able to have while respecting the original material. Moreover, this project was very well received by our sponsor, the Toulon Opera, with whom we will continue to work in my last years of study.
