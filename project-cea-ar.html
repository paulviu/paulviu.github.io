Augmented reality for fusion engineering

September 2019 - September 2022

Part of my projects at the CEA concerns augmented reality design. The objective of this project is to have a superposition of future configurations in the already built ITER building. Therefore, I developed this application with MRTK and Unity for the Hololens 2. The configurations were aligned with the building using a QR code. The user could activate and deactivate some elements, but also observe a predefined configuration. The application had to include animations of the exit of components and the opening of the door. The radioactive dose was calculated on several measurement points of an operator. This allows them to limit the operator's exposure as much as possible. This AR tool can thus help in the design of an environment under construction. The application has tested by 50 people who have told us about possible improvements. ITER General Manager Bernard Bigot was able to test the application and saw in it a potential to improve the design workflow. An article and a video were made about this application by the ITER communication.
