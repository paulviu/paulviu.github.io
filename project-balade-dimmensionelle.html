Balade dimensionnelle

Mars 2023 - January 2024

The Palais de la Découverte is a science museum located in Paris. Opened in 1937, it closed its doors in 2020 for renovations, with the goal of offering a new free-access exhibition. Around twenty multimedia installations covering seven main disciplines (astronomy, life sciences, geosciences, physics, chemistry, computer science, mathematics) were planned for this new exhibition. For each multimedia installation, it was necessary to design a scenario and identify the most suitable technological means to effectively convey the scientific messages defined by the museographers.

While designing an installation aimed at explaining how forces act at different scales, we identified virtual reality as one of the most appropriate technical solutions. However, virtual reality raises a number of questions, including: how can we design a virtual reality experience that is accessible in a free-access museum setting ? I chose this topic as the subject of my professional thesis for my master’s program.

As part of this work, I designed and developed a prototype that was later installed at the Cité des Sciences et de l’Industrie for over a year. This project allowed me to focus my prototype on features and comfort options tailored to various user profiles, including people who are visually impaired, autistic, hearing impaired, or have motor disabilities. All users were able to complete the experience. Here are some of the features I developed and integrated: subtitles, French Sign Language (LSF), interactive audio description, head tracking, hand tracking, voice recognition, and binaural sound.

Thanks to this prototype, I was able to conduct experiments with different specific audiences during testing sessions organized in partnership with associations and the CNAM research lab specializing in gaming and disabilities.
