Virtual reality for fusion engineering

September 2019 - September 2022

During my apprenticeship in a research laboratory of the CEA, I was in the XR development group. In this group, I was able to develop in virtual reality for different applications. Either help to design with an immersion at scale 1 and ergonomic elements with Xsens suits. Or training for the operators in an objective of qualification.
One of the most outstanding projects is the development of a free roaming training with 2 users wearing ventilated suits and xsens sensors. The operators had to perform their tasks with the discomfort of the ventilated suits within a certain time limit so that they were not exposed to radiation for too long. I was also able to participate in the development of haptic arms in order to create a digital twin. We also had the mission to make virtual reality known in our field. That is why I realized realistic renderings in HDRP or lighter simulations in Quest helmets where users can learn more about the machine. I also co-authored an article called "Virtual and Augmented Reality Use Cases for Fusion Design Engineering". This apprenticeship taught me a lot about virtual reality development. I was able to experience many different technologies but also work with a diverse team.
